# Standard Arbitration Rules & Guidelines

## 1. General Principles
1.1 **Fairness**: All parties shall be treated with equality and given a reasonable opportunity to present their case.
1.2 **Impartiality**: The arbitrator (AI or Human) must remain neutral and independent of the parties.
1.3 **Burden of Proof**: The claimant bears the burden of proving their claim by a preponderance of the evidence.

## 2. Contractual Obligations
2.1 **Binding Agreement**: Valid contracts between parties are binding and enforceable.
2.2 **Performance**: Parties must fulfill their obligations as specified in the contract.
2.3 **Breach**: A failure to perform any term of a contract without a legitimate legal excuse constitutes a breach.

## 3. Service Quality & Delivery
3.1 **Standard of Care**: Services must be performed with reasonable care and skill.
3.2 **Timeliness**: Services must be delivered within the agreed timeframe, or within a reasonable time if no time is specified.
3.3 **Defects**: If a service is defective, the consumer may be entitled to a repair, replacement, or refund.

## 4. Payment & Refunds
4.1 **Payment Due**: Payment is due upon completion of services unless otherwise agreed.
4.2 **Withholding Payment**: A client may withhold payment only if the service was not delivered or was significantly defective.
4.3 **Refund Policy**: Refunds are governed by the specific terms of the contract or applicable consumer protection laws.

## 5. Evidence
5.1 **Admissibility**: Relevant evidence including contracts, emails, screenshots, and invoices is admissible.
5.2 **Authenticity**: Parties must vouch for the authenticity of the evidence they submit.
5.3 **Weight**: The arbitrator determines the weight and credibility of the evidence provided.
